Endpoint Journey Map

Endpoint requested a follow-up project in response to the analysis I performed on their user research. My task was to create a journey map to explain the concept of versioning. In a nutshell, versioning refers to the iterative process clinical trials go through in order to be approved for testing with real life participants. They also wanted the journey map to showcase the main features of their product, Flash. Timeline for completion was 4 weeks.

My Process

Research

Similar to the previous project for Endpoint, I needed to gain an understanding of what versioning is and how their product works. I also wanted to know the level of detail that should be included.

Ideation

Stakeholders provided insight on what content to include in the journey map as well as screenshots that were to be replicated in mid-fidelity as reference for the user.

Wireframe

Drafts of the journey map were submitted to Endpoint and Staircase team members for feedback. Job roles, which served as pseudo personas, were included to provide better reference for how Flash helps project managers and design managers manage a clinical trial through the testing lifespan.

Prototype

Endpoint was extremely satisfied with the content along with the level of detail used in each section. Endpoint intends to use the journey map as an artifact for new employees and potential customers.

Conclusion

I gained further confidence in my ability to learn about a complex topic, boil it down to the essential parts, and describe it in a way that someone outside of the project could understand and digest. Mural was an excellent medium for this explanation and along the way I learned how to use its capabilities.
